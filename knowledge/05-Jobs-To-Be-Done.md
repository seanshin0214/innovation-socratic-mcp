# Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

## Category
CREATIVE & INNOVATION

## Description
Understand the underlying "job" customers are trying to accomplish, rather than just their stated needs or demographics.

## When to Use
- Product development
- Market research
- Innovation strategy
- Understanding customer motivation
- Competitive analysis

## Question Sequence (5 questions)

### Question 1/5: THE JOB
What job is the customer trying to get done? What progress are they trying to make?

**Purpose:** Identify the core job

### Question 2/5: CONTEXT
In what situation does this job arise? What triggers the need?

**Purpose:** Understand context

### Question 3/5: CURRENT SOLUTION
What are they using now? What workarounds have they created?

**Purpose:** Identify current alternatives

### Question 4/5: SUCCESS CRITERIA
How will they know they've successfully completed the job? What outcomes matter?

**Purpose:** Define success metrics

### Question 5/5: OBSTACLES
What prevents them from doing this job better? What friction exists?

**Purpose:** Identify opportunities

## Expected Outcome
- Deep customer motivation understanding
- Innovation opportunities identified
- Beyond feature requests
- Predictable growth strategy

## Academic Source
- Christensen, C. (2016). "Competing Against Luck: The Story of Innovation and Customer Choice"
- Harvard Business School

## Tips
- Focus on the job, not the customer segment
- Look for emotional and social jobs too
- One job, many solutions
- Jobs are stable, solutions change
